Global Warming Analysis

Introduction

As you can see, the color of each geographic region changes obviously when you slide the time slider from 1900 to 2010. The average change of some areas are closer to 4 degrees.

According to the research, the average global temperature has increased almost 1 degree centigrade from 1900.

In this analysis, we will analyze the cause of global Warming from three aspects: Population, Greenhouse Gan, & Forest Area.

In the end, we will discuss the impact of global warming.

Population

From the left visualisation, it can be seen that 9 countries with population of more than 100 million were added.

From the right visualisation, we can see that the total population increased around 3.5 billion people. The average temperature increased with the population.

More population means more carbon dioxide, more land occupation, more tree cutting, more ocean fishing, more environmental pollution.

Human has wreaked havoc on the ecological environment which is one of the main reasons of global climate change.

Greenhouse Gas Emission

Due to the concern about the greenhouse effect in recent years, Emissions in the year to March 2021 were 494.2 million tonnes – 5.3% or 27.8 million tonnes lower than the previous year.

From the above visualisations, Except the emission provided by the electricity generate, the following three factors, Transport, Direct Combustion, & Agriculture are closely related to human life.

From the report IPCC 2007, the lifecycle of the greenhouse gas is around 12-50000 year including the lifecycle of CO2 which is 50-200 years.

Forest Area

Through the map above, we can see that the percentage of forest area cover is obviously decrease in some country.

According to the research, deforestation is in fact considered the second major driver of climate change (more than the entire global transport sector), responsible for 18-25% of global annual carbon dioxide emissions.

Every year about 18m hectares of forest – an area the size of England and Wales – is cleared.

Impact

Hotter Days

In 2021, the temperatures in Kuwait exceed 70 degrees Celsius, many areas are approaching 50 degrees Celsius.

This will cause evaporation of wate which increase the water vapour and forest fire which produce plenty of carbon dioxide. This will become an endless cycle.

Arctic to Become Ice-Free

This will cause the sea level increased!

Global sea level has risen by about 8 inches since reliable record keeping began in 1880.

It is projected to rise another 1 to 8 feet by 2100.

It will cause some low-lying coastal areas to be directly submerged, the land area to be reduced & natural disasters to be intensified.

Species Extinction

According to WWF report, One in six species is at risk of extinction because of climate change.

To survive, plants, animals and birds confronted with climate change have two options: move or adapt. Let alone human.

With the amount of living place destruction, moving is becoming increasingly difficult.

Food & Farming

With the climate changes, the growing season will lengthen which means grain production will decrease.

With the population increases, the average amount of food available to the world population will decrease. Not to mention that, according to the statistics from the United Nations, nearly 24 thousand people die of hunger EVERY DAY.
